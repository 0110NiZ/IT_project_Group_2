Origins and Influence

Shandong cuisine, also known as Lu cuisine, is one of the four major cuisines of China. It originated from the Shandong province in the north-eastern region of China, and has a long history that dates back to the Spring and Autumn period.

Sauces and condiments

Shandong cuisine is famous for its fresh, salty, crispy and tender flavors, as well as its exquisite and refined cooking techniques.It emphasizes the use of scallions and garlic to enhance the aroma, and the preparation of clear soup and milk soup to bring out the richness. Shandong cuisine is also known for its variety of seafood dishes, such as braised sea cucumber, fried oysters, and steamed fish. Some of the representative dishes of Shandong cuisine are sweet and sour carp, nine-turn large intestine, Dezhou braised chicken, and crystal peach.

Regional Variations

Because of the geography and culture of Shandong, Lu Cuisine is divided into two main schools: Jinan Cuisine and Jiaodong Cuisine (there is also the unique Kongfu Cuisine). Jinan Cuisine, because of its geography, has a relatively small range of ingredients compared to other Chinese cuisines. However, it is characterized by its careful selection of ingredients, exquisite techniques and time-consuming cooking. Jiaodong cuisine is famous for its seafood, and it is good at combining seafood with other ingredients.
