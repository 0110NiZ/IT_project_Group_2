Origins and Influence

Jiangsu cuisine, one of the eight major cuisines of the Han Chinese, is referred to as Su cuisine. As Su cuisine is close to Zhejiang cuisine, it is collectively known as Jiangsu and Zhejiang cuisine. It is mainly composed of local dishes such as Jinling cuisine, Huaiyang cuisine, Suxi cuisine and Xuhai cuisine. Jiangsu cuisine originated more than two thousand years ago, among which Jinling cuisine originated in the pre-Qin period, when the Wu people were good at preparing grilled fish, steamed fish and fish slices. More than one thousand years ago, duck was already a delicacy in Nanjing. In the Southern Song Dynasty, Jiangsu cuisine and Zhejiang cuisine were the two pillars of "southern food".

Sauces and condiments

Su cuisine is good at stewing, braising, steaming, stir-frying, paying attention to the soup, to maintain the original juice of the dish, the flavor is fresh, thick but not greasy, light but not thin, loose bone without losing its shape, smooth and crisp without losing its taste.
