Origins and Influence

Fujian cuisine is one of the eight major cuisines in China. It is divided into five types according to region: Fuzhou cuisine (centered on Minhou County, Fuzhou, Fujian), Western Fujian cuisine, Putian cuisine, Northern Fujian cuisine and Southern Fujian cuisine. The historical origin of Fujian cuisine. As early as after the "Yongjia Rebellion" during the Jin Dynasty and the Southern and Northern Dynasties, a large number of well-dressed nobles from the Central Plains entered Fujian, bringing with them the advanced science and technology culture of the Central Plains. The mixing and exchange with the ancient Yue culture of Fujian promoted local development. In the late Tang Dynasty and the Five Dynasties, brothers Wang Shenzhi from Gushi, Guangzhou, Henan, led troops to Fujian to establish the "Min Kingdom", which played a positive role in promoting the further development and prosperity of Fujian's food culture.Fujian is located on the southeast coast and is rich in a variety of seafood, which makes the Fujian people good at cooking seafood. Fujian has undulating mountains, dense forests, and a warm climate. Therefore, mountain delicacies, fruits, vegetables, and game are also extremely abundant. The complex and changeable terrain makes Fujian cuisine extremely Diversification. In addition to general seasonings, Fujian cuisine also includes shrimp oil, shrimp paste, sour apricots, etc.; it also has a more prominent "bad" taste, which can be divided into red grains, white grains, grains, etc. In terms of taste, emphasis is placed on freshness, sourness, sweetness, and sourness. Salty and fragrant, the last dish at a banquet is usually seasonal green vegetables, which means "clear vegetables".

Sauces and condiments

Soup making has the reputation of "one soup with ten changes". The most common cooking methods are steaming, steaming, stir-frying, simmering and stewing. Famous dishes include Buddha Jumping Over the Wall, Flat Pork Swallow, Fuzhou Fish Balls, Drunken Pork Ribs, Lychee Pork, Red Pork Ribs, etc. Bad chicken, Fujian fruit, light bad flavored snail slices, boiled sea clams in chicken soup, etc. "Fujian cuisine" is famous for being good at preparing delicacies from the mountains and seas. It has the characteristics of elegant, fresh, tender, mellow and meaningful flavor. Skillful knife skills and originality; exquisite soups and endless variations; unique seasonings; delicate and elegant cooking. These unique features have made Fujian cuisine one of the top eight cuisines in the country. However, outside of Fujian, the home of Fujian cuisine, the reputation of Fujian cuisine is not public. It is not on the same level as Sichuan cuisine, Hunan cuisine, Cantonese cuisine, etc. that are blooming all over the country.
