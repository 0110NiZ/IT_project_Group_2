Origins and Influence

Yue cuisine traces its roots to the Guangdong province of Southern China.It has evolved over centuries through a combination of local traditions, geographical factors, and cultural exchange.The cuisine's prominence extends beyond China due to the large number of Cantonese emigrants.

Sauces and condiments

In Yue cuisine, ingredients such as sugar, salt, soy sauce, rice wine, corn starch, vinegar, scallion and sesame oil suffice to enhance flavour, garlic is heavily used in some dishes. Ginger, chili peppers, five-spice powder, powdered black pepper, star anise and a few other spices are also used, but often sparingly.

Regional Variations

Yue cuisine encompasses the cooking styles of all speakers of Yue Chinese languages in Guangdong.Scholars categorize Guangdong cuisine into three major groups based on dialect:Cantonese: Representing the heart of Cantonese cuisine, especially in Guangzhou.Hakka: Known for its unique flavors and popular in Meizhou and Huizhou.Chaozhou: Found in eastern Guangdong, influenced by Fujian cuisine.
