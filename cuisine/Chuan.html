Origins and Influence

Chuan cuisine is one of the eight traditional Chinese cuisines, an important position in China's culinary history, it takes a wide range of materials, the taste of fresh and mellow and strong, to make good use of spicy, known for its unique cooking methods and rich local flavor, the integration of the characteristics of the parties in the southeast, west and north, and learn from the strengths of the family, and is good at absorbing, good at innovation.

Sauces and condiments

Chuan cuisine features: fresh and mellow, spicy and pungent, a dish of a pattern, a hundred dishes, a hundred flavors.Chuan cuisine is characterized by prominent numbness, spiciness, aroma, freshness, oil, thick flavor, heavy use of "three peppers" (chili peppers, peppercorns, peppercorns) and fresh ginger. Seasoning methods are dry burning, fish, strange flavor, pepper, red oil, ginger, sweet and sour, lychee, garlic and other composite flavors, forming the special flavor of Chuan Cuisine, enjoying the reputation of "one dish, one pattern, one hundred dishes, one hundred flavors".It is internationally known as "Food in China, Taste in Sichuan".

Regional Variations

Chuan cuisine is divided into three schools: Rong faction (on the river gang), Yu faction (down river gang), salt gang faction (small river gang). On the river gang of Chuan cuisine that is to the west of Chengdu, Leshan as the center of the region's Chuan cuisine; small river gang of Chuan cuisine that is to the south of Chuan Zigong as the center of the salt gang of dishes, including Yibin dishes, Luzhou dishes and Neijiang dishes. The lower river gang of Chuan cuisine that is the old eastern Chuan region of Dazhou cuisine, Chongqing cuisine, Wanzhou cuisine as the representative of the Jianghu cuisine.
