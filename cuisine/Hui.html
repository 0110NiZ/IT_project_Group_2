Origins and Influence

Anhui cuisine consists of three styles: the Yangtze River region, Huai River region, and southern Anhui region. Anhui has ample uncultivated fields and forests, so the wild herbs used in the region's cuisine are readily available.

Sauces and condiments

The braised food of Huizhou is a big category, and the "red" of braised food is shown in the use of soy sauce. Soy sauce in Huizhou is made from soybeans, preserving the original colour. The oil used for stir-frying is self-grown and self-pressed rapeseed oil, and a large amount of wood is used as fuel: there is a charcoal fire for warm stewing, a wood fire for rapid burning, and a tree block for slow burning. Traditionally, heavy oil, colour and fire work

Culinary skill

Huizhou cuisine of heavy fire work is a tradition of excellence, it is good at burning, stewing, smoking, steaming and other kung fu dishes, different dishes using different fire control techniques, the formation of crispy, tender, fragrant, fresh unique style of the basic means, which best reflects the characteristics of Huizhou is the slippery burn, stewed and smoked method of raw.
